Carbon Emissions and PM2.5 index

The Environmental Protection Agency divides PM2.5 indicators into four levels: green, yellow, red, and purple. When the concentration of PM2.5 is between 0~35 ug/m3 (micrograms/cubic meter), it is green and normal outdoor activities are allowed; when the concentration is between 36~53 ug/m3, it is yellow, which means moderate, but there are respiratory diseases. It is best to reduce outdoor activities. The colour between 54 and 70 ug/m3 is red, which means the pollution level is high, so it is best to avoid going out. More than 71ug/m3 is purple, so the pollution is very serious.
